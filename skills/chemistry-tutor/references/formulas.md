## Stoichiometry

**Molar Mass:**
$$n = \frac{m}{M}$$

**Molarity:**
$$M = \frac{n}{V}$$

**Dilution:**
$$M_1V_1 = M_2V_2$$

**Percent Composition:**
$$\% = \frac{\text{mass of element}}{\text{molar mass of compound}} \times 100$$

---

## Gas Laws

**Ideal Gas Law:**
$$PV = nRT$$

**Combined Gas Law:**
$$\frac{P_1V_1}{T_1} = \frac{P_2V_2}{T_2}$$

**Dalton's Partial Pressures:**
$$P_{total} = P_1 + P_2 + P_3 + ...$$

**Graham's Law (Effusion):**
$$\frac{r_1}{r_2} = \sqrt{\frac{M_2}{M_1}}$$

---

## Thermodynamics

**Enthalpy Change:**
$$\Delta H = \sum \Delta H_f(\text{products}) - \sum \Delta H_f(\text{reactants})$$

**Gibbs Free Energy:**
$$\Delta G = \Delta H - T\Delta S$$

**Spontaneity:**
- ΔG < 0 → Spontaneous
- ΔG > 0 → Non-spontaneous
- ΔG = 0 → Equilibrium

---

## Equilibrium

**Equilibrium Constant:**
$$K = \frac{[\text{products}]}{[\text{reactants}]}$$

**Reaction Quotient:** Q (same form as K)
- Q < K → Forward reaction favored
- Q > K → Reverse reaction favored
- Q = K → At equilibrium

---

## Acids & Bases

**pH and pOH:**
$$pH = -\log[H^+]$$
$$pOH = -\log[OH^-]$$
$$pH + pOH = 14$$

**Henderson-Hasselbalch:**
$$pH = pK_a + \log\frac{[A^-]}{[HA]}$$

**Weak Acid Dissociation:**
$$K_a = \frac{[H^+][A^-]}{[HA]}$$

---

## Electrochemistry

**Cell Potential:**
$$E°_{cell} = E°_{cathode} - E°_{anode}$$

**Nernst Equation:**
$$E = E° - \frac{RT}{nF}\ln Q$$

**At 25°C:**
$$E = E° - \frac{0.0592}{n}\log Q$$

---

## Kinetics

**Rate Law:**
$$\text{rate} = k[A]^m[B]^n$$

**Half-Life (First Order):**
$$t_{1/2} = \frac{0.693}{k}$$

**Arrhenius Equation:**
$$k = Ae^{-E_a/RT}$$
