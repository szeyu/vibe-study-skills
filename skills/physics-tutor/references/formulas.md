# Physics Formulas & Constants

|----------|--------|-------|
| Speed of light | c | 3.00 × 10⁸ m/s |
| Gravitational constant | G | 6.67 × 10⁻¹¹ N·m²/kg² |
| Planck's constant | h | 6.63 × 10⁻³⁴ J·s |
| Elementary charge | e | 1.60 × 10⁻¹⁹ C |
| Electron mass | mₑ | 9.11 × 10⁻³¹ kg |
| Proton mass | mₚ | 1.67 × 10⁻²⁷ kg |
| Boltzmann constant | k | 1.38 × 10⁻²³ J/K |
| Avogadro's number | Nₐ | 6.02 × 10²³ mol⁻¹ |

## Kinematics (1D Motion)

| Variable | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| x | Displacement |
| v₀ | Initial velocity |
| v | Final velocity |
| a | Acceleration |
| t | Time |

**Equations:**
$$v = v_0 + at$$
$$x = v_0 t + \frac{1}{2}at^2$$
$$v^2 = v_0^2 + 2ax$$
$$x = \frac{1}{2}(v_0 + v)t$$

---

## Forces & Newton's Laws

$$\Sigma F = ma$$
$$W = mg \quad (g = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2)$$
$$f = \mu N \quad \text{(friction)}$$
$$F = -kx \quad \text{(spring)}$$

---

## Work, Energy, Power

$$W = Fd\cos\theta$$
$$KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$
$$PE_g = mgh$$
$$PE_s = \frac{1}{2}kx^2$$
$$P = \frac{W}{t} = Fv$$

---

## Momentum

$$p = mv$$
$$J = F\Delta t = \Delta p$$
$$m_1v_1 + m_2v_2 = m_1v_1' + m_2v_2'$$

---

## Rotational Motion

$$\omega = \frac{\Delta\theta}{\Delta t}$$
$$\alpha = \frac{\Delta\omega}{\Delta t}$$
$$\tau = rF\sin\theta = I\alpha$$
$$L = I\omega$$
$$KE_{rot} = \frac{1}{2}I\omega^2$$

---

## Gravitation

$$F = \frac{Gm_1 m_2}{r^2}$$
$$g = \frac{GM}{r^2}$$
$$PE = -\frac{Gm_1 m_2}{r}$$
$$v_{orbit} = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}$$

---

## Electrostatics

$$F = k\frac{q_1 q_2}{r^2} \quad (k = 9 \times 10^9)$$
$$E = \frac{F}{q} = k\frac{Q}{r^2}$$
$$V = k\frac{Q}{r}$$
$$PE = qV = k\frac{q_1 q_2}{r}$$

---

## Circuits

$$V = IR \quad \text{(Ohm's Law)}$$
$$P = IV = I^2R = \frac{V^2}{R}$$
$$R_{series} = R_1 + R_2 + ...$$
$$\frac{1}{R_{parallel}} = \frac{1}{R_1} + \frac{1}{R_2} + ...$$

---

## Waves

$$v = f\lambda$$
$$T = \frac{1}{f}$$
$$f_n = n \cdot f_1 \quad \text{(harmonics)}$$

---

## Thermodynamics

$$Q = mc\Delta T$$
$$PV = nRT$$
$$\Delta U = Q - W$$
$$e = \frac{W}{Q_H} = 1 - \frac{T_C}{T_H}$$

---

## Modern Physics

$$E = hf = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$$
$$E = mc^2$$
$$\lambda = \frac{h}{p} \quad \text{(de Broglie)}$$
